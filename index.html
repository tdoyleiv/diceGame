Vicksburg, 1863

The American Civil War is in the summer of its second year. Despite early setbacks, the Confederacy has checked every advance of the Union into its sovereign territory. In the Eastern Theatre, the Army of Northern Virginia, led by the brilliant Robert E. Lee has dealt the Army of the Potomac one grievous blow after another. The latest disaster has come against odds of just over two to one numerical superiority in the wilderness near Chancellorsville. Emboldened, Lee marches north, looking to take the war into Pennsylvania and the metropoles of the East. Meanwhile, in the Western Theatre, desperate for victory, the Union sends its Army of the Tennessee, under the dux non grata Ulysses S. Grant, to complete the reconquest of the Mississippi River...

A vital link to desperately needed cereals, cattle, horses, & men of Texas, Arkansas, & Louisiana, Jefferson Davis has ordered Pennsylvania-born John C. Pemberton to hold Vicksburg, the 'Gibraltar of the West' - at all costs.
