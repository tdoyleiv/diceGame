The Siege of Vicksburg

"Your orders, sir?"

Major General, command of the Army of the Tennessee is yours. We have them encircled. However, our scouts report General Johnston's force somewhere north of Jackson and he may arrive any day now. Intelligence estimates put him arriving in 25 days.

Each day, we may choose one strategy, furthering our progress towards taking the city. Let's break down our progress out of a possible 100 siege points. If we obtain 100 siege points the the city will be ours.

Starving the rebel garrison is our safest option, although that will take some time and malaria will spread amongst the men. We also have the Mississippi River Squadron, as well as our own batteries, to shell their position, but secesh is dug in tight and operating the artillery in this heat will tire our boys. Advancing our intrenchments will give us an advantage, but we'll have to divert our manpower to engineering and it'll put us in range of their snipers. If you think we can force a breach, a frontal assault may be ordered, but the casualties may be very costly.

Be wary of maintaining enough men. Should Johnston arrive, we will have to fight a combined force of 60,000 Confederates. The fate of the Union rests on your shoulders and President Lincoln is watching.
